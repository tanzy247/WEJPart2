Milford Sound

A new day. We went on a boat cruise through Milford Sound right up to the end of the Sound, where the water meets the Tasman Sea. We saw some awesome waterfalls and a sea lion colony. It was really impressive to be inside of the Sound and you could feel how big it is.

In the afternoon, after the boat trip, we hiked to Lake Marian.

Otago Peninsula

Again we were absolutely spoilt for wildlife finding more seals and sea lions on the beach and my favourite - penguins!

We went to watch little blue penguins (the smallest breed in the world) return to land after sunset, after a day's hunting at sea. Over 100 penguins came ashore over the evening which was incredible to watch. They cling together at sea to create a raft and wait for the current to wash them ashore and they then waddle up the rocks back to the nests - the cutest!

Mount Cook

Mount Cook is the tallest mountain in NZ. It’s one of the nicest national parks we visited with incredible 360 views even from the car park.

We decided to climb to Mueller Hut for sunrise in order to get great views of Hooker Valley, Hooker Glacier and Mount Cook. It was an early 3am start but it was worth it, as we watched the sun come up over the mountain and reveal stunning views.

Final Day

Our last day in New Zealand. We enjoyed a final real fruit ice-cream which was one of our favourite discoveries whilst in NZ, and we saw a colony of seals with their pups. We were sad to say goodbye to our trusty Toyota that had been our home for the month, but we are excited for our next adventure in Australia.
